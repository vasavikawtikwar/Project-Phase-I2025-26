# Features Guide

## Grammar Checker

### How to Use
1. Go to Grammar Checker page
2. Enter or paste your text
3. Errors appear automatically as you type
4. Click "Apply" button next to any error to fix it instantly
5. Click "Save" to store the analysis (requires login)

### What It Detects
- **Spelling Errors**: "recieve" → "receive"
- **Grammar Errors**: "He don't" → "He doesn't"
- **Punctuation**: Missing periods, incorrect commas
- **Subject-Verb Agreement**: "The cats is" → "The cats are"
- **Homophones**: "your" vs "you're", "their" vs "there"

### Example
**Input:** "She go to the store yesterday"
**Errors Detected:**
- "go" → "went" (tense mismatch)
- Missing period at end

## Voice Converter

### How to Use
1. Go to Voice Converter page
2. Enter a sentence
3. Conversion type (Active/Passive) shown automatically
4. Click "Convert" button
5. Get the converted sentence instantly

### Active to Passive Examples
- "John ate the apple" → "The apple was eaten by John"
- "The cat chased the mouse" → "The mouse was chased by the cat"
- "She wrote the letter" → "The letter was written by her"

### Passive to Active Examples
- "The cake was eaten by Tom" → "Tom ate the cake"
- "The book was written by an author" → "An author wrote the book"

### Supported Tenses
- Simple Present
- Simple Past
- Present Continuous
- Past Continuous

## Ambiguity Detector

### How to Use
1. Go to Ambiguity Detector page
2. Enter your text
3. Ambiguous words and phrases highlighted
4. Hover or click for explanation
5. Clarity score shown (0-100%)

### What It Detects

#### Homophones (Same sound, different meaning)
- **See** (verb: to perceive with eyes) vs **Sea** (noun: body of saltwater)
- **Your** (possessive) vs **You're** (you are)
- **Their** (possessive) vs **There** (location) vs **They're** (they are)
- **Accept** (verb: receive) vs **Except** (preposition: not including)

#### Vague Pronouns
- "It" without clear antecedent
- "This" referring to unclear noun
- "That" needing clarification

#### Awkward Word Order
- "I gave the book to the student with red cover" (which has the cover?)
- "Walking through the park, the statue appeared" (who is walking?)

#### Unclear References
- Long sentences where pronoun reference is ambiguous
- Multiple subjects making the reference unclear

### Clarity Score
- **90-100%**: Very clear, no ambiguity detected
- **70-89%**: Minor ambiguities, easy to understand
- **50-69%**: Some confusion possible
- **Below 50%**: Significant ambiguity, needs rewriting

## View Analysis History

### Accessing History
1. Login to your account
2. Go to any tool page (Grammar Checker, Voice Converter, etc.)
3. Click "View History" or "Past Analyses"
4. See all your saved analyses with timestamps

### What's Stored
- **Date/Time**: When you performed the analysis
- **Original Text**: The text you analyzed
- **Results**: The corrections or conversions
- **Type**: Which tool was used

### Delete History
- Hover over analysis
- Click delete button
- Confirm deletion

## Tips & Tricks

### Grammar Checker
- Paste multiple sentences at once
- Use "Apply" button to quickly fix errors one by one
- Check the error explanation for learning

### Voice Converter
- Use Convert button to swap between active/passive back and forth
- Useful for writing style variation
- Helps with academic papers that require specific voice

### Ambiguity Detector
- Use before sending important emails
- Check clarity score to gauge comprehension difficulty
- Helpful for technical writing

### General
- Create account to save all your work
- Export results for use in documents
- Use for academic writing, professional emails, content creation
